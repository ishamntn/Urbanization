URBANIZATION

What’s the Issue?

Urbanization is the process by which rural areas transform into more urban areas, leading to concentrated populations, infrastructure development, and changes in land use. Today, rapid urban growth has significant consequences for both people and ecosystems.

Learn More View Gallery

Defining Urbanization

Urbanization refers to the increasing percentage of a population that lives in cities and suburbs. In the Philippines, urban growth has accelerated over the past few decades. As of 2023, over 47% of Filipinos live in urban areas, up from 30% in 1990 (Philippine Statistics Authority, 2023).

This shift has led to:

  • Expansion of informal settlements.
  • Increased demand for housing, transportation, and basic services.
  • Conversion of agricultural land into built-up areas.

How Urbanization Affects People & Ecosystems

Impact on Humans

Rapid urbanization often outpaces infrastructure, resulting in:

  • Informal Settlements: Limited access to clean water, sanitation, and healthcare.
  • Traffic Congestion: Longer commute times, increased air pollution.
  • Job Displacement: Rural workers migrating to cities may lack adequate skills for formal jobs.
  • Social Inequality: Wealth gaps between gated communities and slums become more pronounced.

Impact on Ecosystems

Converting natural land into urban areas leads to:

  • Habitat Loss: Encroachment on forests and wetlands disrupts biodiversity.
  • Increased Pollution: Urban runoff flows into rivers, affecting aquatic life.
  • Heat Island Effect: Concrete and asphalt absorb heat, raising local temperatures.
  • Resource Strain: Higher demand for water, energy, and raw materials.

Underlying Factors

The drivers of urbanization include:

  • Economic Opportunity: Cities offer more jobs, especially in services and manufacturing.
  • Educational & Health Services: Better access to universities, hospitals, and clinics.
  • Infrastructure Investments: New roads, public transit, and utilities encourage inward migration.
  • Government Policies: Zoning laws and land-use regulations influence growth patterns.

What To Do: Solutions that effectively and sustainably address rapid urbanization in the Philippines

Urban Green Space Policy

Definition: Metro Manila Ordinance (2018) requiring ≥20% of new-development land be public green space.

Status: Piloted in 4 districts since 2019; city-wide rollout planned.

Procedures: Developers submit landscape plans; quarterly inspections & community planting days.

Results: 15 new parks (2019–2022); +45 ha green cover; –1.2 °C avg heat reduction.

Analysis: Demonstrates policy can deliver measurable cooling and recreation benefits at scale.

Source: Metro Manila City Planning Office (2022).

Barangay Vertical Gardening

Definition: Community program training residents to grow vegetables on walls/rooftops.

Status: Active in 8 barangays since 2021.

Procedures: 4-week workshops, free seed kits, monthly mentoring by agriculturists.

Results: 2,500 households; 30 kg produce/household/month; ~0.5 t CO₂ reduced per household/yr.

Analysis: Turns unused space into micro-farms, boosting food security and small-scale carbon gains.

Source: Dept. of Agriculture & Barangay Dev’t Office (2023).

Bus Rapid Transit (BRT)

Definition: Dedicated-lane bus network in Metro Cebu (launched 2020).

Status: 18 km operational; 10 km under construction as of 2024.

Procedures: Build bus-only corridors, stations every 800 m; unified ticketing, feeder routes.

Results: 150 000 daily riders; –25% commute times; –12% CO₂/pass-km vs. cars.

Analysis: High-capacity transit shifts commuters from cars, cutting travel time & emissions.

Source: Cebu BRT Authority (2024).
